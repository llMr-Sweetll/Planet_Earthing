# Evidence Summary

Last updated: 2026-07-04

This page is the plain-language companion to [evidence-ledger.json](evidence-ledger.json). It distinguishes external empirical evidence from results that Planet_Earthing has not collected yet.

## Project Data Status

Planet_Earthing has collected **no plant-session data yet**. As of this version, the repository contains:

- literature review,
- protocol design,
- data schemas,
- analysis scaffolding,
- CI/CD validation,
- claim-control rules.

It does not contain empirical evidence that plants distinguish a familiar host from strangers.

## Evidence We Can Use

| Evidence ID | What the source supports | What it does not support |
|---|---|---|
| `E001` | Plant electrical signals can be recorded after tactile or damaging stimuli in multiple species. | Familiar-human recognition or emotional intent detection. |
| `E002` | Brief repeated mechanical touch can alter plant morphology, VOCs, and insect responses. | Person-specific plant discrimination. |
| `E003` | Touch-response pathways can affect plant growth regulation. | A detectable host-versus-stranger signature. |
| `E004` | Plants can produce rapid systemic calcium defense signals after wounding. | Human recognition or communication. |
| `E005` | Stressed tomato and tobacco plants can emit informative airborne ultrasonic sounds. | That gentle petting or familiar humans cause ultrasonic emissions. |
| `E006` | Electronic noses can discriminate some plant VOC patterns. | That VOCs recorded during human sessions are plant-only. |
| `E007` | A controlled Backster-style brine-shrimp replication did not find the claimed plant response. | Backster-style primary perception as background evidence. |
| `E008` | The project should avoid consciousness, pain, emotion, and preference claims. | That plants lack measurable signaling. |
| `E009` | Planet_Earthing is not yet an empirical results repository. | Any observed effect from this project. |

## Evidence Required Before Stronger Claims

The project may discuss "familiarity-associated signal differences" only after:

1. the protocol has been preregistered,
2. multiple plants have completed balanced conditions,
3. pressure, voice, VOC, CO2, heat, room entry, and timing confounds have been measured,
4. object/robot-touch and no-touch controls are negative or interpretable,
5. grouped holdout validation succeeds without leakage,
6. confound-only models fail to explain the effect,
7. the result replicates across independent cohorts.

The project may not claim "plants recognize people" from a single plant, row-wise classifier accuracy, uncontrolled host/stranger sessions, or external literature about general plant touch responses.
